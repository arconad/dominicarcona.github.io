About Me

I am a PhD student in Applied Mathematics at the University of Southern California graduating in 2026. My main interests lie in probability, combinatorics, and data science. During my four-year experience as an Analytics Consultant, I applied statistical learning theory to problems facing state and local tax agencies. Previously, I obtained my M.S. in Statistics from USC and my B.A. in Mathematics and Chemistry from Dartmouth College.

Research

My research interests lie at the intersection of probabilistic and algebraic combinatorics. I study mixing models for random walks on the symmetric group, which can be understood using Markov Chains or by applying tools from representation theory.

Publications
  • Dominic Arcona, Representation theory and cycle statistics for random walks on the symmetric group, preprint available here, 2025.
  • Dominic Arcona, Tensor powers of virtual symmetric group representations encoding permutation cycle type statistics, FPSAC 2026 Extended Abstract Submission, 2025.
